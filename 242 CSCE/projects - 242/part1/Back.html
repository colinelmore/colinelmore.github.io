Lat-Pulldowns

During lat pull-downs, the latissimus dorsi muscles, commonly known as the lats, are the primary muscles engaged. These large muscles in the upper back are responsible for the pulling motion as you lower the bar or handle towards your chest. Additionally, the muscles of the shoulders, including the rear deltoids and trapezius, assist in the movement, providing stability and support. The biceps also contribute to the pulling action as secondary muscles. Lat pull-downs are effective for building a strong and well-defined back, improving shoulder strength and stability, and enhancing overall upper body muscular development. They are a fundamental exercise for anyone looking to target their back and improve their posture and upper body strength.
